Inclusion in STEM



What Does it Mean to Collaborate?

To collaborate is to work with others, especially creatively, to achieve a common goal.

Collaboration, as any scienctist, programmer, engineer, or mathematician will tell you, is instrumental to STEM. Most students and professionals regularly work collaboratively on projects, research, and more. It is so important to be able to work effectively with others and find value in the contributions of those around you. The contributions of your groupmates, collegues, and friends can open your eyes to perspectives and ideas you would never consider individually. This is why collaboration is such a prevalent aspect of CRTC's Computer Engineering program.





What does Inclusion Mean?

Inclusion is a practice of ensuring individuals from diverse backgrounds and abilities are actively integrated into a community.

Inclusion in our community means that CRTC is a safe space for all students, regardless of home school and other factors.

Why is Inclusion Important?

Diverse perspectives drive innovation, provide a variety of skills, and help to ensure that tech is made to the needs of our global community. Inclusive environments encourage more creative solutions because of the variety of perspectives and skillsets within the community. In a diverse community, each person can learn from each other and find support in both their technical interest and identity around like minds.



Historically, a variety of barriers have prevented access to science, technology, math, and engineering.
Groups that have faced social and systemic challenges historically include:

  • People of Color
  • Women
  • People of Genders that Aren't Male
  • LGBTQ+ Individuals
  • People with Disabilities
  • Neurodivergent Individuals
  • The Lower Class

and more...

By addressing systemic barriers, we can design a better and more innovative future as a community and create a more equitable STEM community. It is our responsibility as the next generation of science and technology professionals to build a community worth engaging in and growing. To succeed in this goal, we must create inclusive and diverse spaces for our students and professionals.

How to Make an Inclusive Community

Fostering inclusivity is an instrumental goal, but how do we achieve it?

Firstly, we need to understand how diversity in STEM leads to stronger science and innovative solutions. It's also important to educate yourself on STEM barriers and the history of adversity marginalized groups have faced in STEM. Additionally, recognizing one's own baises and privalege is instrumental to removing barriers in STEM.

After you've educated yourself, act! Listen to others, advocate for marginalized groups, and take responsibility for making your community one you can be proud of. Collaboration is extremely important within our communities, as STEM is a team effort, not a solo endeavor. Diverse perspectives on a team help to broaden knowledge and fill in any skill gaps within the project. Learn how to communicate with those you work with to avoid isolating people and create an inclusive group. If you see something, say something! There is no room for exclusion and discrimination in our communities, especially given how important collaboration is to the effectiveness of our work and wellbeing. Don't hesitate to use resources when faced with prejudice or report it when you see it happening to others.





Be intentional with your efforts and support others! STEM is about our collective effort to create solutions, discover, and better the world and our communities should reflect that. Finally, remember that there is no 'right' way to engage in STEM - all perspectives and experiences are valid! The most important thing is our shared passion for science, technology, engineering, and math.
